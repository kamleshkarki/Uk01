On 9 November 2000, Uttarakhand became the 27th state of the Indian Republic, Uttarakhand was earlier called Uttaranchal. The divine site of God still exists in Uttarakhand, so it is also known as the land of the gods, the state is divided into 13 districts, including Kumaon and Garhwal. The winter capital of Uttarakhand is Dehradun, which is the largest city in the state. On 4 March 2020, Gairsain was declared the summer capital of the state. Which comes in Chamoli district. 

In Uttarakhand, a fair is organized on every solstice, which is connected somewhere in Kumaon. Harela, Ghee Sankranti, Khatdua, Ghughutia are the most visited Sankranti in the entire region. And thus the dates in the Gregorian calendar change frequently. Basant Panchami, Shivaratri, Kumauni Holi, Uttarayani, Ram Navami, Dussehra, Rakshabandhan, Janmashtami, Nandaswamy, and Deepawali are auspicious occasions. 

Cholia dance is a very popular dance of the Kumaon region. It is the oldest folk dance of Uttarakhand. 

Kumaoni food is very simple and consists largely of vegetables and pulses. Potato, radish, pumpkin, spinach, bottle gourd, ladyfinger, bitter gourd, and many other vegetables are grown locally from the agricultural population and consumed in various forms. Every year tourists come to Uttarakhand and enjoy nature.
